How It Works

Detection Logic

The system monitors fuel levels from your vehicles' sensors and triggers an alert when all of these conditions are met:

  • Fuel drops significantly - More than the threshold (default 10%)
  • Drop happens quickly - Within the time window (default 30 minutes)
  • Vehicle is parked - Engine/ignition is OFF
  • Vehicle is stationary - Speed is 0, no active trip

Normal fuel consumption while driving does NOT trigger alerts.

Severity Levels

CRITICAL More than 25% fuel drop in less than 10 minutes
HIGH More than 15% fuel drop in less than 20 minutes
MEDIUM More than 10% fuel drop in less than 30 minutes

Example Calculation

Scenario: A truck is parked overnight. At 2:00 AM the fuel level is 75%. At 2:15 AM it drops to 50%.

Calculation:

  • Fuel drop: 75% - 50% = 25%
  • Time elapsed: 15 minutes
  • Vehicle state: Parked, ignition OFF

Result: CRITICAL alert triggered (25% drop in 15 min while stationary)

Tips

  • Adjust the threshold in Settings if you get too many false alerts
  • Fuel sensor accuracy varies - small fluctuations (1-3%) are normal
  • Alerts are deduplicated - you won't get spam for the same vehicle
  • Use Export CSV to download alert history for reporting
